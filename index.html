1. Etiology & Causes

This section explores the fundamental mechanisms initiating tumorigenesis. Tumors arise from uncontrolled cellular proliferation driven by genetic mutations. Interaction with this section reveals the proportional impact of various environmental, genetic, and lifestyle factors on global cancer incidence rates.

🧬 Genetic Factors

Inherited gene mutations (e.g., BRCA1, BRCA2) or acquired mutations over a lifespan significantly alter cell cycle regulation, leading to unchecked growth.

💨 Environmental Carcinogens

Exposure to ionizing radiation, UV light, and chemical agents (asbestos, benzene) directly damages DNA, acting as primary catalysts for malignant transformations.

🍎 Lifestyle & Diet

Tobacco consumption, excessive alcohol intake, and high-processed diets heavily correlate with increased risk profiles across multiple oncological variants.

3. Therapeutic Cures & Modalities

Modern oncology utilizes a multidisciplinary approach to eradication. The graph below illustrates the historical progression of 5-year survival rates, demonstrating the efficacy of combining systemic treatments like chemotherapy with advanced targeted therapies.

💊

Chemotherapy

Systemic administration of cytotoxic drugs designed to destroy rapidly dividing cancer cells, often used in conjunction with other therapies.

🔋

Radiation Therapy

Utilization of high doses of radiation to shrink tumors and eliminate cancer cells by damaging their DNA directly.

🤖

Targeted Therapy

Precision medicine targeting specific genes and proteins that are involved in the growth and survival of cancer cells.

🛡

Immunotherapy

Biological therapy that empowers and modifies the patient's own immune system to recognize and effectively destroy cancer cells.
